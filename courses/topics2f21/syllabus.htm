Prerequisites: Math 230 or 231. Students who have already taken Math 403 are not eligible to enroll in this course.

Course description: This is a course in analysis, which gives a more rigorous underpinning to many of the concepts of calculus that you have studied before. The main topics will include a brief review of calculus, a study of the properties of real numbers, sequences, infinite series, limits, continuity, and differentiation.

Course objectives: After completing the course, students will be able to define, explain, and prove basic theorems about real numbers, sequences, series, and other fundamental concepts of analysis. They will be able to formulate analytic theorems in precise language and give epsilon-delta proofs of these statements.
